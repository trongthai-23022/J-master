📍 Anglia Ruskin University, UK · 29 January 2026
Bamboo could be the world's next superfood, say scientists

Bamboo could be the world's next superfoodsuperfood = siêu thực phẩm. Scientists have been looking at all the studies on bamboo shootsshoots = măng to find out how healthy they are for us. The researchers are from Anglia Ruskin University in the UK. Lead author Professor Lee Smith said: "Our review shows bamboo's clear promise as a possible superfood." This is the first time scientists have put together all existing research to look at bamboo as a food.

Professor Smith and his team found that bamboo shoots can be very beneficialbeneficial = có lợi to our well-beingwell-being = sức khỏe & hạnh phúc. The shoots are low in calories and fat, but high in fibrefibre = chất xơ. This fibre is good for our digestiondigestion = tiêu hóa and helps the body control weight. Bamboo also contains many important vitamins and minerals. These include seleniumselenium = selen, potassium, vitamins A, B6, and E. Scientists say these nutrientsnutrients = chất dinh dưỡng are important for blood sugar levels, blood pressure, and heart health.

The research also shows that bamboo can reduce inflammationinflammation = viêm in the body. It can also reduce oxidative stress. Bamboo contains three types of dietary fibre: cellulose, hemicellulose, and lignin. These have been shown to improve how the bowel works in humans. The scientists also found that bamboo can improve the levels of fat in the blood. This can lower the risk of heart disease.

However, people need to be careful when preparing bamboo. Raw bamboo shoots contain natural toxinstoxins = độc tố. People must boil them before eating to remove these toxins. Bamboo is already commonly eaten in many parts of Asia. Professor Smith said bamboo has great potentialpotential = tiềm năng to become a healthy and sustainablesustainable = bền vững addition to diets all over the world.
