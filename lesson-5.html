📍 Zambia / Oxford University · 14 May 2026
African continent splitting into two parts

There are signs that a hugehuge = rất lớn part of East Africa is starting to break away from the African continentcontinent = lục địa. Geologists (people who study the Earth and its land and rocks) studied a deep crackcrack = vết nứt that runs along a large part of Zambia. The crack is called a riftrift = vết nứt địa tầng. It is a major faultfault = đứt gãy địa chất in the rock that covers Earth's surfacesurface = bề mặt. Geologist Mike Daly of the University of Oxford is studying Zambia's Kafue Rift. He believes there is activity in the rift that suggests a giant piece of land is breaking away. He wrote: "There is evidenceevidence = bằng chứng that the fault boundary of the Kafue Rift is active and therefore the Southwest African Rift Zone is too." He warned that this "may be an early indicationindication = dấu hiệu of the break-up of sub-Saharan Africa".

The land on Earth is constantlyconstantly = liên tục moving. Three hundred million years ago, there was just one huge supercontinentsupercontinent = siêu lục địa. It was called Pangea. Over millions of years, Pangea broke up. Giant areas of land slowly moved across the oceans to form the continents we know today. This movement is called continental driftcontinental drift = trôi dạt lục địa. Rifts are one reason why land masses splitsplit = tách ra apart. The Kafue Rift in Zambia is part of a 2,500-kilometre-long rift zone stretchingstretching = kéo dài from Tanzania to Namibia. The rift is moving at a rate of about 4.7 mm per year. At this rate, it will take millions of years for Africa to split in two. Scientists believe that in 200 million years from now, there could be just one supercontinent again.
